A fictional shipping company

This description of the YODEL codex is from the point of view of the fictional shipping compnay, ACME. In this sinario ACME's best customer Mr Wile E Coyote wants to buy some dehydrated boulders, so that he can catch his favourite prey - road runner. Although YODEL cannot help Mr Coyote directly with getting his lunch they can certinaly help ACME in sending him the parcels that he so desperatly needs.

Our story starts on the 1st of Feburary 2010 just after breakfast. At ACME Geoff in the stores has got into work and has had his cup of coffee when the phone rings, guess who is on the line.

  1. Monday 09:10, a phone call from Mr Wile E. Coyote

    Wile E. Coyote calls ACME to place his order for 300 dehydrated holes. Geoff picks up the phone, he records the sale using his home grown shipping system. The dehydrated boulders come in boxes of 100, and Geoff knows that he will have to create a consignment consisting of three parcels for Mr Coyote.

  2. Monday 09:11, Geoff logs into the codex

    Geoff logs into his shipping application, which is an internal ACME website. His web site offers him a UI which allows him to enter the products he whishes to ship to Mr Coyote. In order for Geoff to request a shipment from the YODEL network his shipping application must login to the codex.

    The URL that represents Geoffs account on the codex is codex.yodel.co.uk/accounts/acme/users/geoff. This returns Geoffs information in an XML format. Geoffs shipping application has a dashboard which shows the most important information to Geoff. To get the Geoff's current list of the current set of YODEL orders it makes another call to the codex using this API. codex.yodel.co.uk/accounts/acme/orders.

  3. Monday 09:15, Geoff creates a new YODEL order

    Geoff wants to creates a new order so he clicks on the New order button. This shows a form where Geoff can enter Mr Coyotes information. Geoff fills in the order information supplied for Mr Coyote. When he is done he hits save.

    His shipping application then posts the order information to the codex. The data for the order is in the POST format and is posted to codex.yodel.co.uk/accounts/acme/orders. This is successfull and redirects the response to the following new order, which has now been given an order reference. codex.yodel.co.uk/orders/272625

  4. Monday 09:16, Geoff creates a consignment

    There are no consignments in this order yet so Geoff hits a "new consignment" button. He is presented with a a form fills for the consignment information. Once this is complete he presses "save" and the form data is posted to the yodel codex here codex.yodel.co.uk/accounts/acme/consignments Again this is successfull and the codex creates a new consignment id and redirects the response to the details for the new consignment codex.yodel.co.uk/accounts/acme/consignments/3456

  5. Monday 09:30, Geoff uses the comments feature to store extra information

    Geoff places alot of orders with yodel and he likes to know what is going where. so he changes the description to "Shipment to Mr Cyote 300 invisible holes" Notice that Geoff can leave comments on the order these are posted to the codex so that they can be shared accross all his systems, and with Yodel. He adds the parcels to the consignment by POSTing parcel objects to the codex with the consignment reference codex.yodel.co.uk/accounts/acme/parcels

  6. Monday 09:47, Mr Coyote wants notifications of his parcel status so he adds a notification

    The order allows Geoff to provide addresses for diferent comunication networks such as email and SMS. Geoffs system allows him to post updates to the order for these. codex.yodel.co.uk/orders/272625 Mr Coyote is very hungery and wants to get on with his plan to catch road runner so he asks Geoff to notified him by email and SMS.

  7. Monday 10:30, My Coyote changes his mind, he only needs 200 dehydrated boulders

    Geoff had previously created a consignment o three boxes each with 100 dehydrated boulders in it. So all geoff has to do is get rid of one parcel. Geoff changes the description and removes one of the parcels from the consignment. His systems acheives this by sending a DELETE request to the address of the parcel in the codex. DELETE codex.yodel.co.uk/parcels/11102236, if the call is successfull it returns the informtion about the consignment and the you will notice the delete in the history. codex.yodel.co.uk/accounts/acme/consignments/3456

  8. Monday 10:45, Geoff needs labels for his parcels

    When the codex is posted parcel details it automatically attempts to get the pre advice. The codex does not only allow users to get information about their parcels it can also generate labels. As with any other information, the label can be accessed via the ReST API. codex.yodel.co.uk/parcels/11102235/label

  9. Monday midnight the van driver is scheduled for the pickup

    The YODEL systems are aware of Geoffs order and they schedule a vandriver to come and pick up the parcels. This can be seen in the order status. codex.yodel.co.uk/accounts/acme/orders/272625

  10. Tuesday 08:45 A van arrives at ACME

    Then the order is collected by driver Mike who has van id 063428. Again this information is stored and is accessible via the order URL codex.yodel.co.uk/orders/272625

  11. Wednesday 10:33 Mr Coyote gets his parcels

    Mr Coyote gets notified by email and text, and on wednesday his parcels arrive. Geoff can tell the status of the delivery by making a call to the codex looking at the parcels URL. codex.yodel.co.uk/parcels/11102236

  12. Wednesday 12:05 Geoffs order status is updated
    When the van driver delivers the parcels to Mr Coyote he scans them. When all the parcels are successfully delivered the order status is updated. Again Geoff can see this by looking at the order. codex.yodel.co.uk/orders/272625

We hope that this example shows you the basic operatio of the YODEL codex. To really get to grips with the codex API take a look at the YODEL codex API.
